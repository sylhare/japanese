# Passive Form - られる

The passive form expresses that the subject is acted upon — something is done *to* them.

- Express that someone receives an action (direct passive)
- Express that something unpleasant happens to someone (adversity passive)
- Speak politely about someone's actions (honorific use)

:::tip Polite Form
For polite speech, replace the final る with ます:
- かかれる → かかれます (is written)
- たべられる → たべられます (is eaten)
- される → されます (is done)
:::

## Group 1 (Godan) — Change to あ-column + れる

Change the final う-sound to its あ-column equivalent, then add れる:

| Ending | Passive Form | Pattern |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| く | かれる | ku → kareru |
| ぐ | がれる | gu → gareru |
| す | される | su → sareru |
| つ | たれる | tsu → tareru |
| る | られる | ru → rareru |
| う | われる | u → wareru |
| ぬ | なれる | nu → nareru |
| む | まれる | mu → mareru |
| ぶ | ばれる | bu → bareru |

:::tip Memory Trick
Godan passive always inserts an **あ** sound before れる:
く → か**れる**, む → ま**れる**, ぶ → ば**れる**.
:::

## Group 2 (Ichidan) — Drop る, add られる

| Ending | Passive Form | Pattern |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| る | られる | ru → rareru |

:::caution Overlap with the potential form
For Ichidan verbs, the passive and potential forms are **identical** (both use られる):
- みられる = "is seen" (passive) OR "can see" (potential)

Context determines the meaning. This ambiguity is why casual speech often uses **ら抜き** (み**れる**) for the potential only — to keep the two forms distinct.
:::

:::info Related: Potential Form
The potential form uses the same られる ending for Ichidan verbs. See [Potential Form - できる](./potential-form) for a full breakdown and comparison.
:::

## Group 3 (Irregular)

| Dictionary Form | Passive Form | Romaji |
|----------------|-------------|--------|
| する | される | suru → sareru |
| くる | こられる | kuru → korareru |

:::warning Special Case: する
する becomes **される**, not すれる or すられる.
- べんきょうする → べんきょう**される** (to be studied)
- せつめいする → せつめい**される** (to be explained)
:::

## Passive Form in Context

**Direct passive:**
> このほんはおおくのひとに**よまれています**。
> *This book is read by many people.*

**Adversity passive (something unpleasant happened):**
> あめに**ふられた**。
> *I got rained on. (lit. I was rained on by the rain.)*

**Praise passive:**
> せんせいに**ほめられました**。
> *I was praised by my teacher.*

**Honorific use:**
> しゃちょうが**いわれました**。
> *The president said... (respectful)*

**Ability (potential reading of られる):**
> ダンスを **おしえられる** ひとを さがしています。
> *I'm looking for someone who can teach dance.*
>
> ぼくが **おしえられます** よ！
> *I can teach you!*

:::info Common Passive Patterns
- **〜に + passive** — agent marker: せんせいにしかられた (was scolded by the teacher)
- **〜によって + passive** — by (formal/written): このうたはビートルズによってつくられた (made by the Beatles)
- **〜ている + passive** — ongoing passive state: ひろくつかわれている (widely used)
- **〜たくない + passive** — don't want to be: わすれられたくない (don't want to be forgotten)
:::
