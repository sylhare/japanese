# Duration Counters

Learn how to express how long something lasts in Japanese.

:::tip Two number systems
Duration counters use Sino-Japanese numbers (いち、に、さん…) except for days, which use native Japanese readings (和語 *wago*) that must be memorized individually.
:::

## Asking How Long

Use **なん** in place of any number to ask "how many":

- なんにち？ — How many days?
  - *nannichi?*
- なんしゅうかん？ — How many weeks?
  - *nanshuukan?*

For a more natural "how long?", use **どのぐらい**:

- どのぐらい にほん に いました か？ — How long were you in Japan?
  - *dono gurai nihon ni imashita ka?*
  - さんかげつ いました — I was there for three months.
    - *sankagetsu imashita*

## ごろ vs ぐらい

Both express approximation but are used differently:

| | ごろ | ぐらい |
|---|---|---|
| **Used for** | point in time | quantity or duration |
| **Meaning** | around (when) | about (how much/long) |
| **Example** | さんじ **ごろ** — around 3 o'clock | さんじかん **ぐらい** — about 3 hours |

- さんじ ごろ に きて ください — Please come around 3 o'clock.
  - *sanji goro ni kite kudasai*
- どのぐらい かかります か？ — About how long will it take?
  - *dono gurai kakarimasu ka?*

:::caution ごろ is for points in time only
**ごろ** cannot be used for durations — only for a specific moment.
- ✓ さんじ ごろ (around 3 o'clock)
- ❌ さんじかん ごろ (about 3 hours) → use **ぐらい** instead
:::

## Days — にち / か

Days use native Japanese readings (和語 *wago*) for 2–10, so each form must be memorized individually. The exception is 1 day (いちにち) which uses the Sino-Japanese reading.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| いちにち | 一日 | ichinichi | 1 day | counter |
| ふつか | 二日 | futsuka | 2 days | counter |
| みっか | 三日 | mikka | 3 days | counter |
| よっか | 四日 | yokka | 4 days | counter |
| いつか | 五日 | itsuka | 5 days | counter |
| むいか | 六日 | muika | 6 days | counter |
| なのか | 七日 | nanoka | 7 days | counter |
| ようか | 八日 | youka | 8 days | counter |
| ここのか | 九日 | kokonoka | 9 days | counter |
| とおか | 十日 | tooka | 10 days | counter |
| じゅうよっか | 十四日 | juuyokka | 14 days | counter |
| はつか | 二十日 | hatsuka | 20 days | counter |

:::tip Same readings as calendar dates
Days 2–10 share readings with calendar dates (2nd, 3rd…). Learning them once covers both!
:::

## Weeks — しゅうかん (週間)

Use **しゅうかん** to count weeks. Only 1 week triggers gemination: いち → いっ before し.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| いっしゅうかん | 一週間 | isshuukan | 1 week | counter |
| にしゅうかん | 二週間 | nishuukan | 2 weeks | counter |
| さんしゅうかん | 三週間 | sanshuukan | 3 weeks | counter |
| よんしゅうかん | 四週間 | yonshuukan | 4 weeks | counter |

:::tip Sound change
1 week: いっ**し**ゅうかん — the double っ appears before し.
:::

## Months — かげつ (ヶ月)

Use **かげつ** to count months as durations. Numbers 1, 6, 8, and 10 trigger gemination before か.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| いっかげつ | 一ヶ月 | ikkagetsu | 1 month | counter |
| にかげつ | 二ヶ月 | nikagetsu | 2 months | counter |
| さんかげつ | 三ヶ月 | sankagetsu | 3 months | counter |
| ろっかげつ | 六ヶ月 | rokkagetsu | 6 months | counter |
| はっかげつ | 八ヶ月 | hakkagetsu | 8 months | counter |
| じゅっかげつ | 十ヶ月 | jukkagetsu | 10 months | counter |

:::caution Sound changes at 1, 6, 8, 10
- 1ヶ月: いっかげつ (not いちかげつ)
- 6ヶ月: ろっかげつ (not ろくかげつ)
- 8ヶ月: はっかげつ (not はちかげつ)
- 10ヶ月: じゅっかげつ (not じゅうかげつ)
:::

## Years — ねん (年)

Use **ねん** to count years. This counter is fully regular — no sound changes.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| いちねん | 一年 | ichinen | 1 year | counter |
| にねん | 二年 | ninen | 2 years | counter |
| さんねん | 三年 | sannen | 3 years | counter |
| じゅうねん | 十年 | juunen | 10 years | counter |

:::tip Fully regular
ねん never changes — just add it after any number with no exceptions.
:::
