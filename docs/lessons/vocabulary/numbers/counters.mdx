# Counters and Frequency

Learn how to express frequency and repetition using Japanese counters.

## What Are Counters?

Japanese uses specific counter words to count different types of objects and express frequency.
This lesson focuses on time-based counters for expressing "how often" something happens.

## Frequency Counters

### Pattern: [time period] に [number] + かい

**Structure:** [time period] に [counter] かい

- **に** = in/per
- **かい (回)** = times, occurrences

### Counting Occurrences

| Number | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji  | English     |
|--------|-------|----------|---------|-------------|
| 1      | 一回    | いっかい     | ikkai   | once        |
| 2      | 二回    | にかい      | nikai   | twice       |
| 3      | 三回    | さんかい     | sankai  | three times |
| 4      | 四回    | よんかい     | yonkai  | four times  |
| 5      | 五回    | ごかい      | gokai   | five times  |
| 6      | 六回    | ろっかい     | rokkai  | six times   |
| 7      | 七回    | ななかい     | nanakai | seven times |
| 8      | 八回    | はっかい     | hakkai  | eight times |
| 9      | 九回    | きゅうかい    | kyuukai | nine times  |
| 10     | 十回    | じゅっかい    | jukkai  | ten times   |

:::tip Sound Changes
- 1回: いっかい (ikkai) - small っ
- 6回: ろっかい (rokkai) - small っ
- 8回: はっかい (hakkai) - small っ
- 10回: じゅっかい (jukkai) - small っ
:::

### How Many Times? なんかい

Use **なんかい (何回)** to ask "how many times?":

- しゅうかん に なんかい？ — How many times per week?
  - *shuukan ni nankai?*
- ねん に なんかい いきます か？ — How many times a year do you go?
  - *nen ni nankai ikimasu ka?*

### Examples

- しゅうかん に いっかい えいが を みます — I watch a movie once a week.
  - *shuukan ni ikkai eiga wo mimasu*
- にち に さんかい は を みがきます — I brush my teeth three times a day.
  - *nichi ni sankai ha wo migakimasu*
- ねん に いっかい にほん に いきます — I go to Japan once a year.
  - *nen ni ikkai nihon ni ikimasu*
- つき に いっかい りょうしん に でんわ します — I call my parents once a month.
  - *tsuki ni ikkai ryoushin ni denwa shimasu*


## Date Counters

### How Many?

Replace the number with **なん** to ask "how many":

- なんにち？ — How many days?
  - *nannichi?*

You can also use **どのぐらい** to ask "how long?" in a more natural way:

- どのぐらい にほん に いました か？ — How long were you in Japan?
  - *dono gurai nihon ni imashita ka?*
  - さんかげつ いました — I was there for three months.
    - *sankagetsu imashita*

### Days

Days use native Japanese readings (和語 *wago*) instead of Sino-Japanese numbers, so almost every form is unique and must be memorized. The exception is 1 day (いちにち) which uses the Sino-Japanese reading.

| English  | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji     |
|----------|-------|----------|------------|
| 1 day    | 一日    | いちにち     | ichinichi  |
| 2 days   | 二日    | ふつか      | futsuka    |
| 3 days   | 三日    | みっか      | mikka      |
| 4 days   | 四日    | よっか      | yokka      |
| 5 days   | 五日    | いつか      | itsuka     |
| 6 days   | 六日    | むいか      | muika      |
| 7 days   | 七日    | なのか      | nanoka     |
| 8 days   | 八日    | ようか      | youka      |
| 9 days   | 九日    | ここのか     | kokonoka   |
| 10 days  | 十日    | とおか      | tooka      |
| 14 days  | 十四日   | じゅうよっか   | juuyokka   |
| 20 days  | 二十日   | はつか      | hatsuka    |

### Weeks

Use **しゅうかん (週間)** to count weeks. Only 1 week triggers a gemination (double consonant): いち → いっ before し.

| English | Kanji | Hiragana  | Romaji     |
|---------|-------|-----------|------------|
| 1 week  | 一週間   | いっしゅうかん   | isshuukan  |
| 2 weeks | 二週間   | にしゅうかん    | nishuukan  |
| 3 weeks | 三週間   | さんしゅうかん   | sanshuukan |
| 4 weeks | 四週間   | よんしゅうかん   | yonshuukan |

### Months

Use **かげつ (ヶ月)** to count months as durations. The numbers 1 (いち), 6 (ろく), 8 (はち), and 10 (じゅう) trigger gemination before か, turning into いっ, ろっ, はっ, and じゅっ.

| English   | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji      |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------------|
| 1 month   | 一ヶ月   | いっかげつ    | ikkagetsu   |
| 2 months  | 二ヶ月   | にかげつ     | nikagetsu   |
| 3 months  | 三ヶ月   | さんかげつ    | sankagetsu  |
| 6 months  | 六ヶ月   | ろっかげつ    | rokkagetsu  |
| 8 months  | 八ヶ月   | はっかげつ    | hakkagetsu  |
| 10 months | 十ヶ月   | じゅっかげつ   | jukkagetsu  |

### Years

Use **ねん (年)** to count years. This counter is fully regular — just add ねん after any Sino-Japanese number with no sound changes.

| English | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji   |
|---------|-------|----------|----------|
| 1 year  | 一年    | いちねん     | ichinen  |
| 2 years | 二年    | にねん      | ninen    |
| 3 years | 三年    | さんねん     | sannen   |
| 10 years | 十年   | じゅうねん    | juunen   |
