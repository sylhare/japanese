# School and Education

School vocabulary is essential for talking about your background, your children, or daily life in Japan. This lesson covers the different levels of school from kindergarten to university, the students at each level, the people and places you find at school, and everyday school life.

## School Levels

Japanese schooling follows a clear ladder. Each level has its own name, and the student name is usually the school name with 〜生 (*-sei*) attached.

```
幼稚園  →  小学校  →  中学校  →  高校  →  大学  →  大学院
(3–6)     (6–12)    (12–15)   (15–18)  (18–22)  (22+)
kinder.   elem.     middle    high     univ.    grad.
```

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Age | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|-----|------|
| ようちえん | 幼稚園 | youchien | 🧒 kindergarten | 3–6 | noun |
| ほいくえん | 保育園 | hoikuen | 👶 nursery, daycare | 0–6 | noun |
| しょうがっこう | 小学校 | shougakkou | 🎒 elementary school | 6–12 | noun |
| ちゅうがっこう | 中学校 | chuugakkou | 📘 middle school | 12–15 | noun |
| こうこう | 高校 | koukou | 📗 high school | 15–18 | noun |
| だいがく | 大学 | daigaku | 🎓 university | 18–22 | noun |
| だいがくいん | 大学院 | daigakuin | 🎓 graduate school | 22+ | noun |
| がっこう | 学校 | gakkou | 🏫 school | — | noun |

:::info 高校 is an abbreviation
**高校 (こうこう)** is short for **高等学校 (こうとうがっこう, koutou gakkou)**. The short form is what people use in everyday conversation.
:::

**Examples:**
- むすめは しょうがっこうに かよっています。 — My daughter goes to elementary school.
  - *musume wa shougakkou ni kayotte imasu.*
- らいねん だいがくに はいります。 — I'll enter university next year.
  - *rainen daigaku ni hairimasu.*

## Students by Level

The student name is built from the school name plus 〜生 (*-sei*). The one exception is kindergarten, where children are called 園児 (えんじ).

| Level | School (場所) | Student (人) |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| 🧒 kindergarten | ようちえん (幼稚園) | えんじ (園児) |
| 🎒 elementary | しょうがっこう (小学校) | しょうがくせい (小学生) |
| 📘 middle school | ちゅうがっこう (中学校) | ちゅうがくせい (中学生) |
| 📗 high school | こうこう (高校) | こうこうせい (高校生) |
| 🎓 university | だいがく (大学) | だいがくせい (大学生) |
| 🎓 graduate school | だいがくいん (大学院) | だいがくいんせい (大学院生) |

:::warning がくせい vs せいと vs じどう
- **学生 (がくせい)** — a student, especially at university or college level.
- **生徒 (せいと)** — a student at elementary, middle, or high school.
- **児童 (じどう)** — a pupil, specifically at elementary school (formal/official term).

Use **学生** for yourself if you're at university, but **生徒** for a high schooler.
:::

**Examples:**
- あには だいがくせいです。 — My older brother is a university student.
  - *ani wa daigakusei desu.*
- いもうとは こうこうせいです。 — My younger sister is a high school student.
  - *imouto wa koukousei desu.*

| クラスメート | | kurasumeeto | 👥 classmate | noun |
| せんぱい | 先輩 | senpai | 👤 senior, upperclassman | noun |
| こうはい | 後輩 | kouhai | 👤 junior, underclassman | noun |
| ともだち | 友達 | tomodachi | 👫 friend | noun |

:::tip 先生 vs 教師
**先生 (せんせい)** is how you address or refer to a teacher politely — it's also used for doctors, lawyers, and other respected professionals. **教師 (きょうし)** names the occupation itself, used when stating a job: ちちは きょうしです (*chichi wa kyoushi desu*) — *My father is a teacher*.
:::

**Examples:**
- たなかせんせいは えいごの せんせいです。 — Mr. Tanaka is the English teacher.
  - *tanaka sensei wa eigo no sensei desu.*
- せんぱいに しつもんしました。 — I asked a senior a question.
  - *senpai ni shitsumon shimashita.*

## Useful Expressions

### Talking About School

- Q: なんねんせいですか？ — What grade/year are you in?
  - *nannensei desu ka?*
- A: だいがく ２ねんせいです。 — I'm a second-year university student.
  - *daigaku ninensei desu.*

- Q: どこの がっこうに かよっていますか？ — Which school do you attend?
  - *doko no gakkou ni kayotte imasu ka?*
- A: ちかくの こうこうに かよっています。 — I go to a nearby high school.
  - *chikaku no koukou ni kayotte imasu.*

:::tip Common Phrases
- いってきます (行ってきます) — I'm off (said when leaving for school)
  - *ittekimasu* — the reply at home is いってらっしゃい (*itterasshai*)
- がんばってください (頑張ってください) — Good luck / Do your best
  - *ganbatte kudasai* — said before a test or exam
:::
