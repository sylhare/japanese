## Cultural Notes

- Japanese uses a different number grouping system than English
- In Japanese, 10,000 (ä¸‡) is the base unit for large numbers, not 1,000
- The pronunciation of some numbers changes when combined (ã‚ˆã‚“ vs ã— for 4, ãªãª vs ã—ã¡ for 7)
- Numbers 4 (å››) and 7 (ä¸ƒ) have alternative readings to avoid sounding like death-related words
