# Basic Numbers

This lesson introduces the core number vocabulary you need to know. Once you've memorized these, you'll learn how to combine them to read any number in the next lesson.

## Numbers 1-10

These are the foundation of all Japanese numbers. Memorize these first!

| Number | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji      | English |
|--------|-------|----------|-------------|---------|
| 1      | 一     | いち       | ichi        | one     |
| 2      | 二     | に        | ni          | two     |
| 3      | 三     | さん       | san         | three   |
| 4      | 四     | よん / し   | yon / shi   | four    |
| 5      | 五     | ご        | go          | five    |
| 6      | 六     | ろく       | roku        | six     |
| 7      | 七     | なな / しち | nana / shichi | seven |
| 8      | 八     | はち       | hachi       | eight   |
| 9      | 九     | きゅう / く | kyuu / ku   | nine    |
| 10     | 十     | じゅう      | juu         | ten     |

### Why Two Readings?

Some numbers have alternative pronunciations:

**4 (四):** よん is preferred; し sounds like 死 (shi - death)
**7 (七):** なな is preferred; しち can be confused with し (4)  
**9 (九):** きゅう is preferred; く sounds like 苦 (ku - suffering)

:::tip Which Reading to Use?
Generally use **よん**, **なな**, and **きゅう** unless you're in specific contexts like:
- Dates: 四月 is しがつ (April)
- Counting specific objects with traditional counters
:::

## Numbers 11-20

Once you know 1-10, Japanese numbers follow a simple pattern:

| Number | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji | Breakdown |
|--------|-------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 11 | 十一 | じゅういち | juuichi | 十 + 一 (10 + 1) |
| 12 | 十二 | じゅうに | juuni | 十 + 二 (10 + 2) |
| 13 | 十三 | じゅうさん | juusan | 十 + 三 (10 + 3) |
| 14 | 十四 | じゅうよん | juuyon | 十 + 四 (10 + 4) |
| 15 | 十五 | じゅうご | juugo | 十 + 五 (10 + 5) |
| 16 | 十六 | じゅうろく | juuroku | 十 + 六 (10 + 6) |
| 17 | 十七 | じゅうなな | juunana | 十 + 七 (10 + 7) |
| 18 | 十八 | じゅうはち | juuhachi | 十 + 八 (10 + 8) |
| 19 | 十九 | じゅうきゅう | juukyuu | 十 + 九 (10 + 9) |
| 20 | 二十 | にじゅう | nijuu | 二 + 十 (2 + 10) |

**Pattern:** じゅう + [1-9] = 11-19

## Place Value Units

These are the building blocks for larger numbers:

| Unit | Kanji | Hiragana | Romaji | Value |
|------|-------|----------|--------|-------|
| Ten | 十 | じゅう | juu | 10 |
| Hundred | 百 | ひゃく | hyaku | 100 |
| Thousand | 千 | せん | sen | 1,000 |
| Ten Thousand | 万 | まん | man | 10,000 |
| Hundred Million | 億 | おく | oku | 100,000,000 |
| Trillion | 兆 | ちょう | chou | 1,000,000,000,000 |

:::info Japanese vs English Grouping
Japanese groups numbers by **4 digits** instead of 3:
- English: 1,000,000 (million)
- Japanese: 100,0000 (百万 - hyakuman - "hundred ten-thousands")
:::

## Quick Examples

Here are some examples of how numbers combine:

- **23** = にじゅう さん (20 + 3)
- **99** = きゅうじゅう きゅう (90 + 9)
- **100** = ひゃく
- **1,000** = せん
- **10,000** = いちまん (1 × 万)
