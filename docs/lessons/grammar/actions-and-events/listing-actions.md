# Listing Actions: たり

Learn how to list **representative examples** of actions or states using the **〜たり〜たり** pattern. Unlike a complete list, it implies the examples are just a sample — "doing things like X and Y."

:::tip Remember
These patterns build on the past plain (**た**) form. Make sure you know your [ta-form conjugations](/docs/lessons/conjugation/past/ta-form)!
:::

## Key Expression

| Hiragana | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|--------|---------|------|
| 〜たり〜たり する | tari tari suru | do things like A and B | expression |

## How It Works

**[Verb た-form] + り + [Verb た-form] + り + する**

Take the **past plain (た) form** of each verb, add **り**, and finish the whole list with **する** (conjugated for tense and politeness). The だ-ending verbs become **だり**:

| Verb | た-form | たり-form |
|------|---------|-----------|
| たべる (eat) | たべた | たべ**たり** |
| のむ (drink) | のんだ | のん**だり** |
| いく (go) | いった | いっ**たり** |
| する (do) | した | し**たり** |

## Listing Example Actions

The actions are **not necessarily in order** — you are giving a flavor of what happens, implying there is more:

- しゅうまつは、ほんを よんだり えいがを みたり します。 — On weekends I do things like read books and watch movies.
  - *shuumatsu wa, hon o yondari eiga o mitari shimasu.*
- パーティーで たべたり のんだり しました。 — At the party we ate, drank, and so on.
  - *paatii de tabetari nondari shimashita.*

:::tip Just examples, not everything
**たり** signals "these are just examples — there's more." That's why it feels close to ending a list with **など** (etc.). Use it when you don't want to give an exhaustive list.
:::

## Repeated or Alternating Actions

With two opposite verbs, **〜たり〜たり** describes going back and forth between states:

- たったり すわったり しないで ください。 — Please don't keep standing up and sitting down.
  - *tattari suwattari shinaide kudasai.*
- あめが ふったり やんだり しています。 — It keeps raining on and off.
  - *ame ga futtari yandari shite imasu.*

## With Adjectives and Nouns

Adjectives and nouns can also take **たり** to contrast alternating states (い-adjective → かったり; な-adjective / noun → だったり):

- ねだんは たかかったり やすかったり します。 — The prices are sometimes high, sometimes low.
  - *nedan wa takakattari yasukattari shimasu.*
- やすみは どようびだったり にちようびだったり します。 — My day off is sometimes Saturday, sometimes Sunday.
  - *yasumi wa doyoubi dattari nichiyoubi dattari shimasu.*

:::warning たり vs て-form
Both can connect actions, but the nuance differs:
- **〜て、〜** lists actions *in sequence* and as a *complete* account: たべて、のみます (eat, then drink).
- **〜たり〜たり** lists actions as *examples*, not exhaustive and not necessarily ordered: たべたり のんだり します (do things like eat and drink).
:::
