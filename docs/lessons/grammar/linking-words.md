# Linking Words

Linking words connect ideas and sentences together.
Mastering these small but powerful words will make your Japanese sound much more natural and expressive.

## Cause and Reason

Use these to explain *why* something happens.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| から | - | kara | because, since (casual) | conjunction |
| ので | - | node | because, since (polite) | conjunction |
| だから | - | dakara | so, therefore | conjunction |
| そのため | その為 | sono tame | for that reason, therefore | conjunction |
| なぜなら | 何故なら | nazenara | because (formal explanation) | conjunction |

**Examples:**

- あめが ふっているから、いえに います。 — Because it's raining, I'm staying home.
  - *ame ga futte iru kara, ie ni imasu.*
- つかれたので、はやく ねました。 — Because I was tired, I went to bed early.
  - *tsukareta node, hayaku nemashita.*
- さむいです。だから、コートを きてください。 — It's cold. So please wear a coat.
  - *samui desu. dakara, kooto o kite kudasai.*

:::tip から vs ので
**から** is more casual and direct. **ので** is softer and more polite — prefer it in formal situations.
- さむいから... → direct, casual
- さむいので... → softer, polite
:::

## Contrast

Use these to express *however*, *but*, or *even though*.

| Hiragana | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|--------|---------|------|
| でも | demo | but, however (casual) | conjunction |
| しかし | shikashi | however (formal) | conjunction |
| けど | kedo | but (casual, spoken) | conjunction |
| けれど | keredo | but, however | conjunction |
| ところが | tokoro ga | however, unexpectedly | conjunction |
| のに | noni | even though, despite | conjunction |
| ても | temo | even if, even though | conjunction |

**Examples:**

- たかいです。でも、かいたいです。 — It's expensive. But I want to buy it.
  - *takai desu. demo, kaitai desu.*
- べんきょうしました。しかし、しけんは むずかしかったです。 — I studied. However, the exam was difficult.
  - *benkyou shimashita. shikashi, shiken wa muzukashikatta desu.*
- いっしょうけんめい れんしゅうしたのに、うまく できませんでした。 — Even though I practiced hard, I couldn't do it well.
  - *isshoukenmei renshuu shita noni, umaku dekimasendeshita.*

:::tip でも vs しかし
**でも** is casual and commonly used in everyday speech. **しかし** is more formal and often used in writing.
:::

## Addition

Use these to add more information — *also*, *moreover*, *in addition*.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| そして | - | soshite | and, and then | conjunction |
| それに | - | soreni | moreover, in addition | conjunction |
| また | 又 | mata | also, again, moreover | conjunction |
| も | - | mo | also, too | particle |
| さらに | 更に | sara ni | furthermore, even more | conjunction |

**Examples:**

- にほんごを べんきょうします。そして、かんじも れんしゅうします。 — I study Japanese. And I also practice kanji.
  - *nihongo o benkyou shimasu. soshite, kanji mo renshuu shimasu.*
- このレストランは おいしいです。それに、やすいです。 — This restaurant is delicious. Moreover, it's cheap.
  - *kono resutoran wa oishii desu. soreni, yasui desu.*
- わたしも いきたいです。 — I also want to go.
  - *watashi mo ikitai desu.*

## Sequence

Use these to order events — *first*, *then*, *finally*.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| まず | 先ず | mazu | first of all, to begin with | adverb |
| それから | - | sorekara | and then, after that | conjunction |
| つぎに | 次に | tsugi ni | next | adverb |
| さいごに | 最後に | saigo ni | finally, lastly | adverb |
| やっと | - | yatto | finally, at last (after effort) | adverb |
| ついに | 遂に | tsui ni | finally, at last (after time) | adverb |

**Examples:**

- まず、てを あらってください。それから、たべてください。 — First, please wash your hands. Then, please eat.
  - *mazu, te o aratte kudasai. sorekara, tabete kudasai.*
- さいごに、しつもんが ありますか？ — Finally, are there any questions?
  - *saigo ni, shitsumon ga arimasu ka?*
- やっと しゅくだいが おわりました。 — I finally finished my homework.
  - *yatto shukudai ga owarimashita.*

## Limitation

Use these to express *only*, *just*, or *nothing but*.

| Hiragana | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|--------|---------|------|
| だけ | dake | only, just | particle |
| しか〜ない | shika~nai | nothing but, only (negative emphasis) | particle |
| ばかり | bakari | nothing but, only, just (excessive) | particle |
| さえ | sae | even, only (emphasizing minimum) | particle |

**Examples:**

- すこしだけ ください。 — Please give me just a little.
  - *sukoshi dake kudasai.*
- にほんごしか わかりません。 — I only understand Japanese (nothing else).
  - *nihongo shika wakarimasen.*
- かれは ゲームばかり しています。 — He does nothing but play games.
  - *kare wa geemu bakari shite imasu.*

:::tip だけ vs しか
Both mean "only" but are used differently:
- **だけ** is neutral and works with positive verbs: みっつだけ あります → There are only three.
- **しか** is negative and must use a negative verb: みっつしか ありません → There are only three (not more).
:::

## Examples and Reference

Use these to give *examples* or *refer back* to something.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| たとえば | 例えば | tatoeba | for example | adverb |
| など | - | nado | and so on, etc. | particle |
| つまり | - | tsumari | in other words, that is to say | conjunction |
| すなわち | 即ち | sunawachi | namely, that is (formal) | conjunction |

**Examples:**

- たとえば、すしや ラーメンなど、にほんりょうりが すきです。 — For example, I like Japanese food such as sushi and ramen.
  - *tatoeba, sushi ya raamen nado, nihon ryouri ga suki desu.*
- かれは ざんぎょうが おおい。つまり、いつも いそがしい。 — He has lots of overtime. In other words, he's always busy.
  - *kare wa zangyou ga ooi. tsumari, itsumo isogashii.*

## Concession and Surprise

Use these to express *actually* or introduce unexpected information.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| じつは | 実は | jitsu wa | actually, in fact | adverb |
| じつに | 実に | jitsu ni | indeed, truly, really | adverb |
| まさか | - | masaka | no way, surely not | adverb |
| もちろん | 勿論 | mochiron | of course, certainly | adverb |

**Examples:**

- じつは、にほんに すんでいます。 — Actually, I live in Japan.
  - *jitsu wa, nihon ni sunde imasu.*
- まさか、そんな ことが！ — No way, something like that!
  - *masaka, sonna koto ga!*
- もちろん、いきます。 — Of course I'll go.
  - *mochiron, ikimasu.*

## Alternative and Choice

Use these to express *or*, *either*, *whether*.

| Hiragana | Kanji | Romaji | English | Type |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|------|
| または | 又は | mata wa | or, either (formal) | conjunction |
| あるいは | 或いは | arui wa | or, possibly (formal) | conjunction |
| か | - | ka | or (in lists) | particle |
| どちらか | - | dochira ka | either one (of two) | expression |

**Examples:**

- バスまたは でんしゃで いきます。 — I'll go by bus or train.
  - *basu mata wa densha de ikimasu.*
- コーヒーか おちゃを のみますか？ — Will you drink coffee or tea?
  - *koohii ka ocha o nomimasu ka?*

## Putting It All Together

Here are some example sentences combining multiple linking words:

- まず、にほんごを べんきょうしました。それから、かんじも ならいました。やっと、じょうずに なりました。
  - *Mazu, nihongo o benkyou shimashita. Sorekara, kanji mo naraimashita. Yatto, jouzu ni narimashita.*
  - First, I studied Japanese. Then, I also learned kanji. Finally, I became good at it.

- じつは、にほんごが すきです。でも、むずかしいです。だから、まいにち れんしゅうします。
  - *Jitsu wa, nihongo ga suki desu. Demo, muzukashii desu. Dakara, mainichi renshuu shimasu.*
  - Actually, I like Japanese. But it's difficult. So I practice every day.

- このえいがは ながいです。それに、むずかしいです。しかし、とても おもしろいです。
  - *Kono eiga wa nagai desu. Soreni, muzukashii desu. Shikashi, totemo omoshiroi desu.*
  - This movie is long. Moreover, it's difficult. However, it's very interesting.
